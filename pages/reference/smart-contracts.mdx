## Overview

Kyan's smart contracts are deployed on Arbitrum and handle trade settlement, margin custody, and liquidation execution. All trading operations are signed off-chain using [EIP-712 signatures](/reference/eip-712-signatures) and settled on-chain through the clearing house.

## Clearing House

The Clearing House is the core contract in Kyan's architecture. It handles:

- **Deposits and withdrawals** -- collateral is deposited into and withdrawn from the clearing house. All margin is held in the contract.
- **Trade settlement** -- matched orders are settled on-chain by submitting the signed order pair to the clearing house.
- **Margin calculations** -- the contract enforces margin requirements and prevents under-collateralized positions.
- **Liquidation execution** -- when an account's margin falls below the maintenance requirement, the clearing house executes liquidation.

## Insurance Fund

The Insurance Fund acts as a backstop for the exchange's solvency:

- **Funding source** -- a portion of liquidation penalties is directed to the insurance fund after each liquidation.
- **Loss absorption** -- when a liquidated account's losses exceed its remaining collateral, the insurance fund absorbs the difference.
- **Socialized losses** -- if the insurance fund is depleted, remaining losses are socialized across profitable accounts proportionally.
