# Your First Perps Trade

<Callout type="tip">
  New to perps? Read [Perpetual Futures](/concepts/perpetuals) first.
</Callout>

This tutorial guides you through opening a leveraged perpetual futures position on Kyan. You will choose a market, set leverage and size, review margin requirements, and learn how to monitor your position once it is live.

<Callout type="info">
  **Prerequisites**

  - A connected wallet with USDC deposited -- see [Getting Started](/tutorials/getting-started) if you have not done this yet
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Select the Perps Market

Select the **Perps** tab from the top navigation bar and choose the underlying asset you want to trade. For this tutorial we will use **ETH-PERP**. This opens the perpetual futures trading view for that market.

### Choose Your Direction

Decide whether you are bullish or bearish on the asset:

- **Long (buy)** -- you profit when the price goes up
- **Short (sell)** -- you profit when the price goes down

Click the corresponding button on the order ticket.

### Set Leverage

Adjust the leverage slider to choose your multiplier, from **1x** up to **20x**. Higher leverage amplifies both gains and losses and moves your liquidation price closer to your entry. If you are new to perpetuals, start with low leverage (2x--5x) until you are comfortable with the mechanics.

### Enter Order Details

Set your position size in contracts or USD notional value. Choose between a **market order** (fills immediately) or a **limit order** (fills at your specified price or better). If you select a limit order, enter your desired price.

### Review Margin Requirements

Before submitting, review the key risk parameters displayed in the order ticket:

- **Initial margin** -- the collateral required to open the position
- **Maintenance margin** -- the minimum collateral to keep the position open
- **Liquidation price** -- the price at which your position would be forcibly closed

Make sure you are comfortable with the liquidation price relative to current market conditions.

### Confirm and Execute

Review the full order summary, then click **Confirm**. Approve the transaction in your wallet. Once filled, your position will appear in the **Positions** panel.

</Steps>
