# Your First Options Trade

<Callout type="tip">
  New to options? Read [Options Basics](/concepts/options-basics) first.
</Callout>

This tutorial walks you through buying a call option on Kyan from start to finish. By the end you will have a live position and know how to monitor it.

<Callout type="info">
  **Prerequisites**

  - A connected wallet with USDC deposited -- see [Getting Started](/tutorials/getting-started) if you have not done this yet
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Select the Options Market

Select the **Options** tab from the top navigation bar and choose the underlying asset you want to trade. For this tutorial we will use **ETH**. This opens the options chain for that asset.

### Choose an Expiry

The options chain displays available expiration dates across the top. Select your preferred expiration date from the available maturities. Nearer-dated options are cheaper but decay faster; further-dated options give the trade more time to play out.

### Pick a Strike Price

Select the strike price for your call option based on your market outlook. A strike slightly above the current market price (out-of-the-money) costs less premium but requires a larger move to become profitable. A strike at or below the current price (at- or in-the-money) costs more but has a higher probability of finishing in profit.

### Set Your Order

Enter the number of contracts you want to buy. Choose between a **market order** (fills immediately at the best available price) or a **limit order** (fills only at your specified price or better). The order ticket will display:

- **Premium** -- the total cost of the option
- **Max profit** -- unlimited for calls
- **Max loss** -- limited to the premium paid
- **Breakeven** -- the price the underlying must reach at expiry

### Confirm and Submit

Review the full order summary, then click **Buy**. Confirm the transaction in your wallet. You can watch the order fill in real time on the order book.

### Monitor Your Position

Open the **Positions** tab to track your option. Here you can see:

- **P&L** -- current unrealized profit or loss
- **Greeks** -- delta, gamma, theta, and vega
- **Time to expiry** -- countdown to expiration

</Steps>
