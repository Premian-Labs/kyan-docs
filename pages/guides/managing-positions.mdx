Once you have open trades, the Positions panel becomes your primary workspace for monitoring performance, adjusting exposure, and closing out positions.

## Viewing open positions

The **Positions** panel displays all open positions with the following key metrics:

| Column | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Instrument | The contract name (e.g., ETH-PERP, ETH-28MAR-3000-C) |
| Size | Number of contracts held, with side indicated (long or short) |
| Entry price | Your volume-weighted average fill price |
| Mark price | The current market price used for P&L and margin calculations |
| Unrealized P&L | Profit or loss if you closed at the current mark price |
| Liquidation price | The price at which your position would be liquidated |

Use the toggle at the top of the panel to switch between **Options** and **Perpetuals** views.

## Adjusting a position

### Increase size

Place a new order in the **same direction** as your existing position. The new fill is averaged into your entry price.

### Decrease size

Place an order in the **opposite direction** for a partial amount. For example, if you are long 10 ETH-PERP and want to reduce by half, sell 5 ETH-PERP.

### Change leverage on perpetuals

Click the position row to expand it, then select **Adjust Leverage**. Increasing leverage reduces the margin allocated to the position but moves your liquidation price closer. Decreasing leverage does the opposite.

## Setting stop-loss and take-profit

Click any position row to expand it, then select **Add TP/SL**:

- **Take profit** -- set a price at which the position automatically closes to lock in gains.
- **Stop loss** -- set a price at which the position automatically closes to limit losses.

Both can be attached simultaneously to the same position. Each creates a conditional order that triggers when the mark price crosses your specified threshold.

## Closing a position

### Market close

Click the **Close** button on the position row. This sends a market order to close the entire position immediately at the best available price.

### Limit close

Place a limit order in the opposite direction at your target exit price. You get price control, but the order may not fill immediately if the market does not reach your price.

### Partial close

Enter a quantity smaller than your full position size when placing the closing order. The remainder stays open as a reduced position.

## Position history

The **History** tab records all closed positions and individual trade fills. Use it to:

- Review past trades and their realized P&L.
- Analyze your trading performance over time.
- Export trades as CSV for record-keeping or tax reporting by clicking the download icon in the top-right corner of the History panel.
