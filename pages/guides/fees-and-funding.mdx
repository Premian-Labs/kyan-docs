Kyan operates with a transparent fee structure designed to offer competitive rates across all markets. This guide covers trading fees, perpetual funding rates, settlement mechanics, and gas costs.

## Trading fees

Kyan uses a maker-taker fee model. Fees are calculated as a percentage of the notional value of each trade.

| Type | Maker | Taker |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Options | 0.02% | 0.05% |
| Perpetuals | 0.01% | 0.03% |
| Combo orders | 0.02% | 0.04% |

<Callout type="info">
Kyan uses a maker-taker model. Limit orders that add liquidity pay maker fees. Market orders and limit orders that cross the spread pay taker fees.
</Callout>

## Funding rates

Perpetual futures do not have an expiry date, so funding payments are used to keep the perpetual price anchored to the spot index price.

**How funding works:**

- Funding payments occur **every 8 hours** at 00:00, 08:00, and 16:00 UTC.
- When the funding rate is **positive**, longs pay shorts. This happens when the perpetual trades at a premium to the index.
- When the funding rate is **negative**, shorts pay longs. This happens when the perpetual trades at a discount to the index.

**How rates are calculated:**

The funding rate is derived from the time-weighted average premium or discount of the perpetual price relative to the spot index over the 8-hour funding interval. A clamp is applied to prevent extreme rates during periods of high volatility.

**Where to view current rates:**

Navigate to the **Market Data** page and open the **Funding** tab. This displays the current rate, the next funding timestamp, and a historical chart of past rates.

## Settlement

Options on Kyan are **cash-settled in USDC** at expiry:

- **In-the-money options** are automatically exercised at expiry. The settlement amount equals the difference between the strike price and the settlement price (the index price at the expiry timestamp).
- **Out-of-the-money options** expire worthless with no action required and no settlement fee.
- Settlement proceeds are credited to your account balance immediately after the expiry window closes.

## Gas fees

All trading on Kyan involves smart contract interactions on-chain, which require gas:

- **Order submission and cancellation** incur gas costs that vary with network congestion.
- **Batched transactions** minimize costs by grouping multiple operations into a single on-chain transaction where possible.
- Gas estimates are displayed in the order confirmation dialog before you submit, so there are no surprises.
