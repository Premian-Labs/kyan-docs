title: "How to Use Portfolio Margin"
description: "Enable portfolio margin to reduce margin requirements and improve capital efficiency."
---

<Callout type="info">
Understand the theory first: [Portfolio Margin explained](/concepts/portfolio-margin).
</Callout>

Portfolio margin evaluates risk across your entire portfolio rather than on a per-position basis. By recognizing hedges and offsets between correlated positions, it can significantly reduce your total margin requirements compared to isolated margin mode.

## Enable portfolio margin

<Steps>

### Navigate to margin settings

Open **Settings > Margin Mode** from the account menu in the top-right corner.

### Switch to portfolio mode

Toggle from **Isolated** to **Portfolio** margin mode.

### Confirm the risk disclosure

Review and accept the risk disclosure that explains how portfolio margin differs from isolated margin.

</Steps>

## Check your margin requirements

Open the **Portfolio Summary** panel to view your current margin state:

- **Total margin used** -- the dollar amount of collateral currently locked across all positions.
- **Initial margin** -- the collateral required to open new positions. You cannot open a trade if it would push your usage above this threshold.
- **Maintenance margin** -- the minimum collateral required to keep existing positions open. Dropping below this level triggers a margin call.
- **Margin ratio** -- total margin used as a percentage of your account equity. Lower is safer.

Set up threshold alerts in **Settings > Notifications** (e.g., alerts at 70%, 80%, and 90% margin ratio) so you are never caught off guard.

## Optimize your portfolio

- **Hedged positions reduce margin.** A long ETH perpetual paired with a short ETH call creates a natural hedge. The margin engine recognizes this offset and requires less collateral than the sum of the two positions in isolation.
- **Diversify across uncorrelated assets.** Spreading exposure across different underlyings reduces concentration risk and can improve margin efficiency.
- **Avoid concentrated single-direction exposure.** A portfolio that is entirely long or entirely short receives little benefit from portfolio margining.
- **Monitor your margin ratio continuously.** Stress-test parameters widen during volatile markets, which can increase margin requirements rapidly even without new trades.

## Handle margin calls

When your margin ratio reaches the maintenance threshold:

1. **You receive a notification** via the channels configured in Settings > Notifications.
2. **Add collateral** by depositing additional funds to your account to bring the ratio back within safe levels.
3. **Reduce positions** by closing or reducing your most margin-intensive trades. Focus on positions with the highest individual margin contribution.
4. **If you take no action**, the liquidation engine will begin closing positions automatically, starting with the largest contributors to portfolio risk.

<Callout type="warning">
Portfolio margin can increase risk. If the portfolio risk engine determines elevated risk, margin requirements may increase without warning.
</Callout>
