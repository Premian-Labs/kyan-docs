# Portfolio Margin

Portfolio margin is a risk-based margining system that evaluates your entire portfolio as a single unit, rather than requiring separate collateral for each individual position. When your positions offset each other's risk, the system recognizes that and lowers your total margin requirement accordingly.

This approach rewards hedged, well-constructed portfolios with significantly better capital efficiency.

## Isolated vs portfolio margin

Under **isolated margin**, every position is treated independently. Each one has its own collateral bucket, its own liquidation threshold, and its own risk. If you hold a long ETH perp and a long ETH put, the system does not recognize that the put protects you against downside moves on the perp. You need full margin for both.

Under **portfolio margin**, the system evaluates all positions together. That same long ETH perp paired with a long ETH put creates a defined-risk position. The maximum loss is bounded, so the margin requirement drops substantially compared to holding each position in isolation.

This difference becomes dramatic as portfolios grow in complexity. A trader running a delta-neutral strategy with multiple options legs and a perp hedge might need 3-5x less margin under portfolio margin than under isolated margin.

## How it works

Kyan's risk engine runs a series of stress-test scenarios across your entire portfolio. These scenarios simulate what would happen to your portfolio value under various adverse price moves, such as the underlying dropping 10%, rising 15%, or volatility spiking.

For each scenario, the engine calculates the projected portfolio loss. Your margin requirement is set to the **worst-case loss** across all scenarios, plus a buffer. This ensures the protocol holds enough collateral to cover the most extreme plausible outcome.

The scenarios are recalculated continuously as market conditions and your positions change, so your margin requirement adjusts in real time.

## Benefits

- **Lower margin requirements.** Hedged positions require less collateral because the system recognizes offsetting risk. Capital that would be locked up under isolated margin is freed for other uses.
- **Greater capital efficiency.** You can run larger or more complex strategies with the same amount of collateral.
- **Strategy flexibility.** Multi-leg option strategies, delta-hedged positions, and cross-product portfolios become practical without excessive capital lockup.

## Risks

Portfolio margin is powerful, but it introduces risks that isolated margin does not:

- **Correlated liquidation.** Under isolated margin, only the failing position gets liquidated. Under portfolio margin, a margin shortfall can trigger liquidation across your entire portfolio.
- **Correlation breakdown.** Portfolio margin assumes that hedges work as expected. In extreme market conditions, correlations between positions can break down, and the hedges may not offset losses as modeled.
- **Complexity.** Managing a portfolio-margined account requires understanding how changes to any single position affect your total margin and risk profile.

<Callout type="warning">
Portfolio margin is designed for experienced traders. The increased capital efficiency comes with increased complexity and risk. Make sure you understand how your positions interact before enabling portfolio margin.
</Callout>
