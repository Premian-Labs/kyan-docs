# About Kyan

Kyan is a decentralized derivatives exchange built on Arbitrum, offering both options and perpetual futures trading. It combines on-chain settlement with off-chain order matching to deliver the performance of a centralized exchange while preserving the transparency and self-custody guarantees of DeFi.

The platform is designed for traders who need capital-efficient margining, institutional-grade risk management, and the ability to execute complex multi-leg strategies atomically.

## Core architecture

Kyan's architecture is built around several interconnected systems, each handling a distinct part of the trading lifecycle.

### Portfolio margin

Rather than requiring separate collateral for every position, Kyan uses a portfolio margin system that evaluates all of your positions together. When positions offset each other's risk, the system recognizes the hedge and reduces your total margin requirement accordingly.

At the heart of this is the **RiskMatrix**, a scenario-based risk engine that runs 43 distinct market scenarios against your portfolio. Each scenario simulates a different combination of price moves and volatility shifts. Your margin requirement is set to the worst-case projected loss across all scenarios, ensuring the protocol holds enough collateral without over-margining hedged positions.

This approach gives well-constructed portfolios significantly better capital efficiency than isolated margin systems.

### Account system

Every wallet on Kyan can create multiple **sub-accounts**, each functioning as an independent trading environment with its own margin, positions, and orders. Within each sub-account, positions are organized into **per-asset portfolios** grouped by underlying asset (BTC, ETH, ARB).

This structure provides natural risk isolation. A volatile position in one sub-account cannot affect the margin health of another, and risk is calculated independently per portfolio within each sub-account.

### Risk calculation

The RiskMatrix is the engine that determines how much margin each portfolio requires. It evaluates 43 market scenarios that cover a range of adverse price movements and volatility shifts for each underlying asset.

For every scenario, the engine computes the projected loss across all positions in a portfolio. The margin requirement equals the worst-case loss, plus a safety buffer. This calculation runs continuously as prices move and positions change, keeping margin requirements current at all times.

### Liquidation engine

When a portfolio's margin ratio falls below the maintenance threshold, Kyan's liquidation engine activates a three-phase process designed to minimize disruption:

1. **Delta hedging.** The system first attempts to reduce portfolio risk by liquidating perpetual positions to flatten the portfolio's net delta exposure.
2. **Portfolio decomposition.** If delta hedging is not sufficient, the system breaks the portfolio into individual positions and liquidates the riskiest ones.
3. **Full liquidation.** If the account remains underwater after the first two phases, all remaining positions are liquidated. Any negative equity is absorbed by the insurance fund.

This graduated approach protects both the trader and the exchange by resolving margin shortfalls with the least possible market impact.

## Orderbook and matching

Kyan uses a hybrid model: orders are matched off-chain for speed and settled on-chain for transparency. The off-chain matching engine handles order placement, cancellation, and matching at high throughput, while the Arbitrum blockchain serves as the final settlement layer for all trades.

This design eliminates the latency and gas costs of fully on-chain orderbooks while preserving verifiable settlement.

## RFQ system

The request-for-quote (RFQ) system is built for institutional and block-size trades. A trader submits a quote request specifying one or more instruments and sizes, and market makers respond with firm prices. The trader can then accept a quote, and the entire trade executes atomically.

RFQs support multi-instrument requests, meaning you can get a single price for a package of positions (such as an options spread plus a perp hedge) and execute the entire package in one transaction. This eliminates leg risk for complex strategies.

## Combo trades

Combo trades allow multi-leg options strategies to execute as a single atomic order. Instead of placing separate orders for each leg of a spread or straddle and risking partial fills, you submit the entire structure as one order. Either all legs fill or none do.

This is critical for strategies where the individual legs are only attractive as part of a package. A bull call spread, for example, only makes sense if both the long and short call fill together.

<Callout type="info">
Kyan settles all products in USDC. There is no physical delivery of any underlying asset. Profits, losses, margin, and fees are all denominated in USDC.
</Callout>
